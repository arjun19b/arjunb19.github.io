Procedural Content Generator (Unity)

Context: Semester project focusing on designing a seed‑based 3D environment and creature generator with modular meshes and AI‑driven variations.

This document outlines the problem space, design goals, and engineering decisions. It explains the mesh generation pipeline, street graph creation (4‑way, T‑junction, turns, dead ends), and a system for building footprints, facades, and elevations. It also summarizes a creature generation subsystem with swappable parts and curved surfaces.

Developed a seed-driven creature generator that automatically produces diverse bodies and palettes; renders distinct creatures per run with only a main random seed as input. Implemented custom curved-surface modeling and swappable body-part variants to achieve variety in geometry, size, and color.

Key Design Choices

  • Seed‑driven determinism for reproducible worlds.
  • Polygon mesh approach for buildings with parametric facades.
  • Composable street tiles enabling complex layouts.

Results

  • Interactive city layouts generated in seconds.
  • Consistent performance with scalable complexity.
